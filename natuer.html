My Village

Our village Muhammad Khan Notakani is a peaceful and beautiful village known for its fertile land and agricultural activities. The natural environment of our village is very pleasant, with green fields spreading far and wide. Agriculture is the backbone of our village, and most of the people depend on farming for their livelihood.

One of the major crops grown in our village is rice (paddy), which is cultivated during the summer season and requires plenty of water. Due to suitable soil and climate, rice production in our area is very good.In addition to rice, wheat is also an important crop of our village and is grown during the winter season. Wheat is a staple food and plays a vital role in fulfilling the food needs of the villagers. Along with these major crops, farmers also grow a variety of vegetables such as potatoes, onions, tomatoes, spinach, chilies, okra, peas, radish, and cauliflower. These vegetables are used for daily consumption and some are sold in nearby markets, which helps farmers earn extra income.The people of Muhammad Khan Notakani are hardworking, simple, and closely connected to the land. They use traditional as well as modern farming methods to improve crop production. Agriculture not only supports the economy of our village but also strengthens unity and cooperation among the people. Our village is a true example of rural life where nature, hard work, and simplicity exist together in harmony.

TOMATO

Tomato is an important vegetable crop grown in many regions. Farmers first sow tomato seeds in nurseries, and after some time, young plants are transplanted into the fields. Tomato plants require regular watering, fertilizer, and protection from pests and diseases. After two to three months, the plants produce ripe red tomatoes. Tomatoes are harvested carefully and are widely used in cooking and making sauces.s

Rice

Rice is one of the most important food crops in many countries. It is mainly grown in the summer season and needs a large amount of water to grow properly. Farmers first prepare the land and grow rice seedlings in nurseries. Later, these young plants are transplanted into flooded fields. With regular care and irrigation, the crop grows well. After a few months, when the rice plants become mature, the crop is harvested. Rice is a staple food and provides energy to millions of people.

WHEAT

Wheat is a major cereal crop grown mostly during the winter season. Farmers prepare the soil carefully and sow wheat seeds directly into the fields. This crop needs moderate water, sunlight, and fertilizer for healthy growth. Over time, the plants turn green and later become golden when fully mature. After about four to five months, the wheat crop is harvested. Wheat is widely used to make flour, bread, and many other food items.

OKRA

Okra is a popular green vegetable grown in warm weather. Farmers sow okra seeds in soft and well-prepared soil. The plants need regular watering and care to grow properly. After about forty to fifty days, the plants start producing green pods. Okra is harvested frequently to get fresh and tender vegetables. It is healthy and commonly used in daily cooking.
