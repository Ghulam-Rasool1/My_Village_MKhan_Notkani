According to local belief and oral history, the village of Fatha Bagh is home to an ancient and respected Shrine of Shahid Aaradin and Shahid Jamardin. This shrine is believed to belong to the time of Mughal Emperor Akbar and Birbal. The two brothers were known as religious and innocent men who spent their lives reciting the Holy Quran and following a simple and pious way of life in the city of Emperor Akbar. It is believed that during that period, Emperor Akbar’s daughter developed feelings for Shahid Aaradin. This matter remained hidden for some time, but later a servant woman informed the emperor about it. The news was presented in a way that created anger and misunderstanding. As a result, Emperor Akbar ordered severe punishment for both brothers, and they were unjustly executed. Because of this act, the two brothers are remembered as martyrs (shahids) by the people..

Shahid Aradin, Shahid Jamardin

After their death, the area of Fatha Bagh is believed to have suffered sorrow and destruction. Over time, the resting place of Shahid Aaradin and Shahid Jamardin became a Shrine, symbolizing sacrifice and faith. People from the village and nearby towns still visit this shrine to pray and remember their courage and devotion. The story of the two brothers is now a part of the local history and teaches lessons of honesty, faith, and patience.

Budhal Shah

In our village, there is an old historical place known as Miro ka Kubh. This place is associated with the Mir people and is believed to be very ancient. It contains the graves of respected elders from the Mir community. Large domes and strong stone-built structures can be seen there, showing the skill of old-time builders. These stone buildings have stood for many years. Miro ka Kubh is considered a symbol of history, honor, and respect for ancestors.

Syed Masoom Shah

In our village, there is an old and ancestral cemetery known as Syed Masoom Shah. This is a very ancient burial place where our elders and grandfathers were laid to rest many years ago. The cemetery holds great importance for our village history and family heritage. It is also believed that a mosque appeared there over time, which the villagers consider a sign of blessing. People visit this place with respect, remembering their ancestors and offering prayers. The Syed Masoom Shah cemetery is a symbol of faith, tradition, and the deep roots of our village.
